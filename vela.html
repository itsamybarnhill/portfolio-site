Background

Context

Water sport enthusiasts such as surfers, kayakers, and snorkelers want their activities on the water to be relaxing and fun, not stressful. To avoid any mishaps, it’s important to check the winds, waves and weather conditions before heading out. This can help us avoid hazards and be fully prepared for a day full of surfing, diving, or sailing.

Problem

Weather apps do exist for water sport enthusiasts, however what is currently available doesn’t do a great job of displaying complicated weather data in an accessible, easy to understand manner.

Hypothesis

Watersport enthusiasts such as sailors, surfers, and divers need a way to receive accurate weather data presented in an easy to comprehend and visually attractive way, so that they can make informed decisions about participating in water sports.

Goal

Create a product that offers current conditions information, graphs, and future forecasts all beautifully displayed and easy to read at a glance, geared specifically for the needs of water sport enthusiasts.

Research

Competitive Analysis

As part of my research, it was important to understand competitors in the same space were doing well, and discover what opportunities might exist for my project. I selected two apps with similar features, Waterspeed and Windy. To analyze, I created a marketing profile for each competitor, prepared a SWOT analysis, and performed a UX and features analysis of each competitor.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with three individuals sourced from my personal network. The interviews were conducted remotely over Zoom.

Questions

  • How much time do you normally spend checking a mobile weather app?
  • What parts of a weather app do you use the most?
  • What device would you normally use when checking the weather?
  • Tell me about a time you were frustrated with a weather app?
  • What is your goal when you are checking a weather report?
  • How much time do you spend on water sports per week?
  • Tell me about a great experience you had while doing water sports
  • Tell me about a bad experience you had while doing water sports

Quotes

“It’s always an awesome feeling when I wake up in the morning, check the surf report and see that the conditions are going to be just right” -User Interviewee

“Knowing the shorebreak really well is so important for safety, especially where I live. Staying aware of the ocean conditions is super important, they change fast” -User Interviewee

“I check weather about five minutes at a time, usually once in the morning, and throughout the day, to see what’s happening on radar” - User Interviewee

Discoveries

  • Users reported they are looking at specific conditions reports the most, including wave swell size, tide report, and wind direction. Other specific reports mentioned were temperature, live radar, the uv index, and time of sunrise/sunset.
  • Overall, the users I interviewed reported checking their mobile weather app of choice at least once per day, in approximately five minute increments. Interviewees reported that they check the weather more than once on days when they are planning to do water sports, usually closer to 2-3 times per day.
  • Users reported they generally use a mobile phone to check the weather, occasionally a tablet or iPad, and even more occasionally a desktop. Users also reported they do generally bring their phone or device with them, but not necessarily while actively participating in water sports. One interviewee reported they sometimes use a waterproof case with lanyard while paddle boarding
  • Interviewees reported that a good day on the water consisted of ideal weather conditions, not forgetting anything they needed for the day like snacks/sunscreen, avoiding overcrowded beaches, and avoiding jellyfish.
  • Interviewees reported a bad day or a “no go” day on the water consisted of going out to surf and not catching any waves, overcrowding at beaches/shore breaks, and dangerous conditions such as rip currents or sharks.

Analyze

User Personas

I developed two user personas based on the data I had gathered. Raymond, a 28 year old realtor who has been surfing all his life, and Shelby, a 29 year old teacher who has just recently taken up paddle boarding as a hobby.

User Journey Maps

After analyzing, I felt I had gained a deeper understanding of what the User Personas needed. Now for the fun to begin! I put on my creative thinking cap and sat down to brainstorm some ideas to help meet those needs. As part of this, I developed two user journey maps for personas Raymond and Shelby.

Summarizing Insights

  • Water sports contribute to user’s quality of life by providing them an outlet for fun and/or stress relief
  • Users want to be aware of water and atmospheric conditions so they can plan to participate when conditions are optimal 2) Maximize safety
  • Users felt that mainstream weather apps provide good data, but may not provide data in an engaging manner that is specific to user’s needs
  • Users don’t necessarily want to develop a deep proficiency with interpreting weather maps and data

Targeted Feature Ideas

  • A feature allowing users to set weather condition parameters and receive notifications when those parameters are met.
  • Accurate and straightforward reports of weather data including Air, Water, and Atmospheric conditions
  • Ability to save and favorite locations for water sports
  • Alerts to notify of hazards such as incoming bad weather, marine life reports, algae or pollution.
